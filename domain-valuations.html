The real value isn't in resale — it's in what these domains produce.

A single domain like hotspringsrvpark.com costs $12 to register. If it generates one lead that closes at $7,500 commission, that's a 625x return. The domain's resale value of $2,000 is almost irrelevant compared to its lead-generation value over 5, 10, or 20 years.

Valuation Methodology

"With Website" values reflect the estimated resale value of each domain with a professionally built, SEO-optimized website — the kind Paul builds: custom design, lead capture forms, Schema.org markup, Google Analytics, mobile-responsive, cross-linked to the network, branded to The Bergeron Group. A developed site with content and authority is worth 4–5x a bare domain. After 12+ months of Google indexing, lead history, and traffic data, values increase further.

These estimates are based on:

  • Comparable domain sales — GoDaddy Aftermarket, Sedo, and NameBio data for geographic real estate domains
  • Keyword commercial intent — domains matching high-intent buyer searches (e.g., "[city] homes for sale") command premium prices
  • Transaction value of the niche — domains in high-commission niches (luxury, lake, commercial, land) are worth more because each lead is worth more
  • Domain length and memorability — shorter, cleaner domains score higher
  • Competition for the keyword — less online competition = more valuable domain (owner finance, RV parks, barndominiums)
  • Geographic exclusivity — there is only one hotspringsrvpark.com. Once registered, no competitor can ever have it.

These are estimated fair market values for private-party sales to a motivated buyer in the real estate industry. Actual resale prices depend on market conditions, buyer urgency, and negotiation. Values do not include the SEO authority, lead history, or website content that would significantly increase value once sites are live and ranking.
