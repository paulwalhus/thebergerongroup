That's why, down the road — once we can both see the leads flowing and the tracking data is solid — I'd love to explore a small performance marketing bonus that keeps me invested in making every site better, month after month, year after year.

What this might look like (just as a conversation starter): A flat marketing services bonus — something like $400-600 — paid only when a tracked lead from one of my sites results in a closed deal. Not a percentage, not a commission share. A flat marketing fee for a measurable result, the same kind of arrangement companies like Zillow, CINC, and HomeLight use every day. It's a standard marketing services model.

Why This Would Be Good for You

  • You'd never pay it unless you made money first. No closed deal from my site = no bonus. Zero risk.
  • It keeps me invested for the long haul. I'd be constantly optimizing your sites, adding content, improving rankings, testing what works — not because you asked me to, but because every lead that converts means we both win.
  • It's a fraction of what you pay elsewhere. CINC charges a monthly subscription. Zillow charges per lead. OpCity and HomeLight take 25-35% of your commission. A flat $400-600 on a $7,500+ commission is 5-8% — and only when it works.
  • It only counts leads we can prove came from my sites. That's what the source tracking is for. If the lead didn't come from a site I built, it doesn't apply.
